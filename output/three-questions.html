Context & Intent
Domenic is a new VP at Teradata navigating a competitive battlefield against Snowflake and Databricks. These questions are not interrogations — they are diagnostic instruments designed to surface where the real work lives and where your methodology creates immediate value.
01
D — Data Layer
Data — Insight Edge
Where is Teradata generating data that leadership is not yet turning into decisions?
Teradata has been in the data business since 1979. They have more data infrastructure than almost any company on the planet. But infrastructure and intelligence are not the same thing. Most organizations at Teradata's scale are data-rich and insight-poor at the decision layer — meaning the data exists but the governance layer that routes it to the right decision-maker at the right time is broken or missing entirely.

Domenic, when you look across your new roadmap at Teradata — where are you seeing the biggest gap between the data your platform generates and the decisions your leadership team is actually making because of it?

Listen For
Specific business units where insight latency is costing them
Your Value
AI Governance layer that routes insight to decision faster
Salt Angle
Engineering the intelligence pipeline from data to decision
02
A — Agility Layer
Agility — Execution Edge
Which assumptions inside Teradata's AI strategy have the shortest expiration date right now?
This is your signature concept applied directly to his world. Every enterprise AI strategy is built on assumptions about market position, customer behavior, and competitive dynamics. Snowflake and Databricks are not standing still. The assumptions Teradata built its current roadmap on six months ago may already be expiring. A new VP walking into that environment needs to know which assumptions to pressure-test first before committing resources.

As you build out your roadmap, which of Teradata's current strategic assumptions about AI adoption feel most time-sensitive to you — and what would it mean if those assumptions expired before the roadmap delivered?

Listen For
Where he already sees the strategy calcifying before it ships
Your Value
Criteria Expiration framework identifies which assumptions need refresh cycles
Salt Angle
Rapid prototyping and validation before assumptions lock in
03
R+E — Risk & Evolution
Risk + Evolution — Disruption Readiness
Is Teradata positioned to be the disruptor in its space — or is it engineering a sophisticated response to disruption that has already happened?
This is the most provocative question and the most important one. Snowflake and Databricks have already moved the market. Teradata has the legacy infrastructure, the enterprise relationships, and the data depth that neither competitor can replicate quickly. But being the biggest is not the same as being the boldest. If Teradata's AI roadmap is primarily defensive — built to close the gap with competitors rather than open a new one — then it is already behind before it ships. A new VP has a unique window to shape that direction before it calcifies.

When you look at where Teradata's AI roadmap is heading versus where Snowflake and Databricks are moving — do you see Teradata as the one setting the pace, or responding to it? And where in your roadmap is there room to change that answer?

Listen For
Whether he sees the offensive opportunity or is locked in defensive framing
Your Value
CES Delta scoring reveals where Teradata leads and where it lags vs competitors
Salt Angle
Engineering the evolution layer — building systems that lead rather than follow
